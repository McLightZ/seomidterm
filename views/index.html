Domain Name Strategy: The reason for a domain name strategy is to proactively ensure your brand online by diminishing the chance for others to utilize your brand name in their online exercises, particularly, in their site URLs.

Linking Strategy: alludes to the way toward getting external pages to link to a page on your site. It is one of the many strategies used in search engine optimization (SEO).

Meta Description Tags Strategy: The meta description tag in HTML is the 160 character snippet used to summarize a web page’s content. In this post, we will look at how search engines use meta descriptions, what the top SEO blogs say about meta descriptions & whether they still use them, and reasons why you should use them.

Title Tag Strategy: A title tag is an HTML element that specifies the title of a web page. Title tags are displayed on search engine results pages (SERPs) as the clickable headline for a given result, and are important for usability, SEO, and social sharing. The title tag of a web page is meant to be an accurate and concise description of a page's content. Your title tag determines (with a few exceptions) your display title in SERPs, and is a search visitor's first experience of your site.

Alt Tags Strategy: alt tags provide a description of an image for those who have disabilities and rely on screen readers to navigate the Web. This is a great example of the importance of writing Web content for people and not for search engine robots. Usability and relevance to your users should always be at the forefront of your efforts. In SEO terms, alt tags make it possible for search engines to properly read and understand what an image is conveying. In other words, it’s one more element on your site for search engines to crawl and another chance for you to infuse your keywords (assuming the image is relevant to your keywords). It will also help you think more strategically about the images on your page to make sure you’re maximizing your targeted keywords.

Search Indexing Strategy: Search engine indexing is the process of a search engine collecting, parses and stores data for use by the search engine. The actual search engine index is the place where all the data the search engine has collected is stored. It is the search engine index that provides the results for search queries, and pages that are stored within the search engine index that appear on the search engine results page.

On-page and Off-page Techniques

The difference between on page and off page SEO comes down to control. In on page SEO, you can target a search term through keyword research, create a page, and tweak the content for the best results. You have complete control over your optimization efforts. Off Page SEO is different. It relies more on promotion. These include link building, blogging, social media, and other off page SEO techniques to build awareness. Control is in the hands of your community.

On-page Technique


On-page SEO is the demonstration of streamlining diverse parts of your site that influence your search engine rankings. It's stuff that you have control over and can change on your own site. It includes: Incorporating selective keyword naturally into title tags, meta descriptions, heading tags, alt text, etc. Blog posts and page copy that is written and optimized with quality, Clean and formatting page URLS, Optimized page load speed, Google authorship incorporated, Social sharing integration within your content and etc.

Off-page Technique


Off-page SEO focuses on increasing the authority of your domain through the act of getting links from other websites. It includes: Creating a high quality, natural backlink profile (aka having other high quality/authoritative sites link to your site naturally), Social sharing signals, Social bookmarking (Stumbleupon, Reddit) and etc.

HTTP Status Codes


HTTP status codes are standard response codes given by web site servers on the Internet.

1xx: Informational - This means that the request is recieved and the process is continuing.

2xx: Success - This means that the request was successfully recieved, understood, and accepted.

3xx: Redirection - This means that the client must make another action to complete the request

4xx: Client Error - This means that the client's request has an error or that it cannot be fulfilled for some reason.

5xx: Server Error - This means that the server failed to fulfill a valid request.
